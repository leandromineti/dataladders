# References

While conducting the research on the topic, the references were grouped in three clusters:

- **General career ladders**: with references on general career management concepts.
- **Technical career ladders**: narrowing the scope on technical career management concepts.
- **Data career ladders**: focusing on data-related career management concepts.

## General career ladders

- **[Carter, G. W., Cook, K. W. & Dorsey, D. Career paths: charting courses to success for organizations and their employees. (Wiley-Blackwell, 2009)](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Career+Paths%3A+Charting+Courses+to+Success+for+Organizations+and+Their+Employees-p-9781405177320)**. The chapter 1 gives a conceptual introduction on what a career ladder is and why it is important. Chapter 2 and 3 provide toolkits for building career paths.

## Techincal career ladders

- **[Fournier, C. The manager’s path: a guide for tech leaders navigating growth and change. (O’Reilly, 2017)](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-managers-path/9781491973882/)**. On chapter 9 there is a section called “Writing a Career Ladder” with interesting advice on the topic.
